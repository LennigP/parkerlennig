Senior Design: Autonomous Walking Robot

School Design, Manufacturing Project

Objective: As mechanical lead in a team of five, we were to develop, manufacture, and test an autonomous walking robot, utilizing leg linkages and drivetrains from our Sponsor, Professor J. Michael McCarthy. We were to develop a robot that had strong repeatability in its movement, whilst utilizing LiDAR to allow the robot map its environment, and autonomously navigate around obstacles to reach a target position.

Results: I developed the robot on Solidworks and manufactured the entire robot, adding design changes such as brass rods for increased rigidity in both the chassis and the upper leg linkages, as well as mounts for the LiDAR sensor, Jetson Orin Nano controller, and more. The robot was successful in its goal of autonomous navigation, and was both presented and demonstrated at Uc Irvine's Annual Design Review.

Pneumatic Powered Robot

School Design Project

Objective: As mechanical lead of my team, I was assigned to design and manufacture a pneumatic powered robot with the ability to steer and propel itself along an obstacle course. The robot was limited in size to be smaller than the diameter of the tire utilized on its top, and must use compressed air as its energy source for propulsion.

Results: I designed a wooden chassis to hold a rack-and-pinion drive system, with a 3D-printed steering system that was controlled with a servo. The robot was able to move forward at 1.2 ft/s, as well as have a steering range of 110 degrees.
